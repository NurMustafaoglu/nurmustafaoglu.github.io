Recent Research Projects

Antibody purification

The objective of this project is to design a novel affinity chromatography techniques for purification of monoclonal and polyclonal antibodies from cell culture media and ascites fluids. This method utilizes the nucleotide-binding site (NBS) that is located on variable domain of Fab region nearly all antibodies to facilitate capturing antibodies on a membrane affinity column.
Publications:

  • Nur Mustafaoglu, Tanyel Kiziltepe, Basar Bilgicer (2016). Antibody purification via affinity membrane chromatography method utilizing nucleotide binding site targeting with a small molecule. Analyst, 141, 6571-6582.

Blood brain barrier

Objective: Designing of enhanced liposomal delivery systems for crossing the blood brain barrier (BBB).

Antibody drug conjugation

Objective: Developing an antibody drug conjugate system in order to use it as selective targeting agent for treatment of cancer and autoimmune diseases with enhanced therapeutic efficacy.

Site-specific functionalization of antibodies and Fab fragments for enhanced diagnostic applications

Objective: Immobilization of antibodies and their Fab fragments in an oriented manner to small scalled diagnostic surfaces such as microfluidic devices and nanoparticles while preserving their antigen binding activity and structure stability.

Nano Science and Nano Engineering M.Sc Thesis Project

Langmuir Blodgett assembly of peptide functionalized nanoparticles onto quartz surfaces and their characterization, October 2011 - 2012
Advisors: Prof. Dr. Mustafa URGEN, Prof. Dr. Candan TAMERLER

Abstact: A bio-enabled fabrication method based on Langmuir Blodgett (LB) technique was developed to create gold nanoparticle patterns on silicate based surfaces. We first designed chimeric peptide which is composed of gold and silica specific domains as a molecular linker between gold nanoparticles and the silicate surface. We then optimized the solution properties to prevent the aggregation of the gold nanoparticles and to promote the LB film formation of the peptides onto the substrate. The binding affinity of AuBP4-QBP2 to nanoparticles is confirmed by absorption spectra. Based on several analyses including UV/Vis spectra, FT-IR and Surface Pressure/Area isotherms, EtOH:ultrapure water systems was chosen. Peptide mediated LB film formation of the AuNPs on silicate based glass were characterized using AFM and FEG-SEM. The developed peptide based Langmuir Blodgett technique provides a platform to develop organic–inorganic hybrid structures over large areas using environmentally friendly conditions.
